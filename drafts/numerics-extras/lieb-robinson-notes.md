# Lieb–Robinson bound for the 1D Heisenberg chain — notes for qf-vr4

These notes back the appendix figure that visualises Heisenberg-picture
operator spreading $A(t) = U^\dagger A\, U$ on a 1D antiferromagnetic
Heisenberg chain ($n = 9$, seed $= 46$, $Z+ZZ$ disorder, periodic) at the
two TimeDomain-relevant times $t_\max$ and $t_\mathrm{median}$ pulled from
`_precompute_labels` for the canonical CKG cell at each $\beta_\mathrm{phys}
\in \{0.25, 0.5, 1.0\}$, and overlays the Lieb–Robinson lightcone for the
1D XXX chain. The script `scratch_jump_spread_n9_LR.jl` writes the
figure and a BSON sidecar; this note records the literature inputs and the
empirical resolution of the operator-spread question.

## Setting

Per-bond Hamiltonian in our convention ($J = 1$, Pauli matrices — not
$S = \sigma/2$):

$$
h_{i,i+1} \;=\; X_i X_{i+1} + Y_i Y_{i+1} + Z_i Z_{i+1} \;=\; 4\,(\boldsymbol{S}_i \cdot \boldsymbol{S}_{i+1}).
$$
<!-- \label{eq:hbond} -->

The full chain Hamiltonian (before the qf-bphys rescaling) is

$$
H_\mathrm{phys} \;=\; \sum_i h_{i,i+1} \;+\; (\text{small } Z + ZZ\ \varepsilon\text{-disorder},\ \varepsilon = 0.1),
$$

extensively scaling in $n$. The algorithm-frame Hamiltonian stored in
`ham.data` is $H_\mathrm{alg} = H_\mathrm{phys}/R + s\,\mathbb{1}$ with
$R = $ `ham.rescaling_factor` chosen so the spectrum lands in $[0, 0.45]$.
Unitary evolution under $H_\mathrm{alg}$ by $t_\mathrm{alg}$ equals unitary
evolution under $H_\mathrm{phys}$ by $t_\mathrm{phys} = t_\mathrm{alg}/R$ (the
shift contributes only a global phase that cancels in $A(t) = U^\dagger A U$).

The per-bond operator $h_{i,i+1}$ has eigenvalues $\{+1, +1, +1, -3\}$
(triplet vs.\ singlet), so the operator norm of a single bond is

$$
M \;:=\; \|h_{i,i+1}\|_\mathrm{op} \;=\; 3 \qquad (\text{our }J = 1\text{ Pauli convention}).
$$

The $\varepsilon = 0.1$ disorder adds a per-site $Z$ term and a per-bond
$ZZ$ term, raising $M$ by at most $0.1 \cdot (1 + 1) = 0.2$, which we absorb
into the safety factor of the LR bound below.

## Lieb–Robinson bound

We use the Bravyi–Hastings–Verstraete 2006 / Hastings 2010 form for
finite-range spin Hamiltonians on a graph $G$ with maximum coordination $Z$
[Bravyi, Hastings, Verstraete 2006][CITE: BravyiHastingsVerstraete2006];
[Hastings 2010][CITE: Hastings2010LesHouches]:

$$
\| [A_x(t), B_y(0)] \|_\mathrm{op}
\;\le\; 2\,\|A\|\,\|B\|\,|\partial x|\,\bigl(e^{v_\mathrm{LR}\,|t|} - 1\bigr)\,e^{-d(x, y)},
$$
<!-- \label{eq:lr-bhv} -->

with $v_\mathrm{LR} = 2 e M Z$ for the cleanest BHV form. For a 1D
nearest-neighbour chain $Z = 2$ (each site neighbours its two adjacent
sites), so

$$
v_\mathrm{LR}^\mathrm{BHV} \;=\; 2 e M Z \;=\; 12\,e \;\approx\; 32.6.
$$

(With the looser 1D convention $Z = 1$ the constant drops to $6 e \approx
16.3$; the factor-of-two ambiguity is a coordination-number convention and
is irrelevant for the qualitative lightcone we draw — the spinon velocity
below is the tighter empirical bound that the data actually saturates.)

The BHV bound is rigorous but loose. The *empirical* Lieb–Robinson
velocity saturated by 1D spin-$\tfrac{1}{2}$ XXX is given by the maximum
group velocity of the Bethe-ansatz spinon dispersion. For the standard
chain $H = J \sum_i \boldsymbol{S}_i \cdot \boldsymbol{S}_{i+1}$ with
$\boldsymbol{S} = \boldsymbol{\sigma}/2$, the spinon dispersion is

$$
\varepsilon(k) \;=\; \frac{\pi J}{2}\,|\sin(k)|,
$$

so the max group velocity is $v_\mathrm{sw} = \pi J / 2$
[Faddeev, Takhtajan 1981][CITE: FaddeevTakhtajan1981]. The LR-saturation
argument is in [Calabrese, Cardy 2006][CITE: CalabreseCardy2006Quench].

In our Pauli convention $h_{i,i+1} = 4\,\boldsymbol{S}_i \cdot
\boldsymbol{S}_{i+1}$ (equivalent to $J = 4$ in the $\boldsymbol{S} =
\boldsymbol{\sigma}/2$ convention), giving

$$
v_\mathrm{LR}^\mathrm{tight} \;=\; v_\mathrm{sw} \;=\; \tfrac{\pi \cdot 4}{2} \;=\; 2\pi \;\approx\; 6.28 \qquad (\text{1D XXX, our units}).
$$
<!-- \label{eq:vlr-tight} -->

This is the slope we use for the lightcone overlay in the appendix figure;
the BHV bound $12 e \approx 32.6$ is quoted in the caption but not drawn (it
is always non-binding on a chain of length $n = 9$ for the times the
simulator visits, so visualising it adds nothing). For a sharper
prefactor-by-prefactor bound see [Wang, Hazzard 2020][CITE: WangHazzard2020LR].

## $\beta$-independence

The LR bound is a statement about the dynamics generated by $H$ — there
is no $\rho_0$, no temperature, no dissipator. $\beta$ enters this
appendix *only* through the choice of which TimeDomain time labels the
simulator considers physically relevant (via the OFT prefactor
`oft_domain_prefactor` and the filter cutoff `filter_time_cutoff` inside
`_precompute_labels` / `_precompute_data` for `Config{Lindbladian,
TimeDomain, KMS}`). The underlying $H$, and therefore the spreading of
$A(t)$, is $\beta$-independent.

Concretely: the CKG canonical smooth-Metropolis cell at $(n,
\beta_\mathrm{phys})$ sets $\sigma_\mathrm{alg} = 1/\beta_\mathrm{alg} =
1/(\beta_\mathrm{phys}\,R)$ (per CLAUDE.md), and the OFT time kernel is
$f(t) = \exp(-\sigma^2 t^2)$ (CKG Gaussian filter). The truncated time
grid surviving `_truncate_time_labels_for_oft(tolerance = 10^{-12})` has
effective half-width

$$
t_\max^\mathrm{alg} \;\approx\; \frac{\sqrt{\log\bigl(\text{prefactor}/10^{-12}\bigr)}}{\sigma_\mathrm{alg}},
$$

with $\text{prefactor} = \sqrt{\sigma\,\sqrt{2/\pi}/(2\pi)}$. The
physical-time half-width is $t_\max^\mathrm{phys} = t_\max^\mathrm{alg} /
R$. *All three* $\beta_\mathrm{phys}$ cells we report share the same
Hamiltonian, so the LR envelope is the same function of $t_\mathrm{phys}$
across panels and only the $t$-values move.

## Site-weight envelope on the figure

The figure plots the per-site operator weight

$$
w_k(t) \;:=\; \frac{1}{d}\,\Bigl[\|A(t)\|_\mathrm{HS}^2 \;-\; \tfrac{1}{2}\,\|\mathrm{Tr}_k[A(t)]\|_\mathrm{HS}^2\Bigr],\qquad d = 2^n.
$$
<!-- \label{eq:wk-def} -->

By the Pauli decomposition $A(t) = \sum_P c_P\,P$ this equals

$$
w_k(t) \;=\; \sum_{P : P_k \neq \mathbb{1}_k} |c_P|^2,
$$

i.e.\ the sum of squared coefficients over Pauli strings with a
non-identity Pauli at site $k$. The normalisation has $w_k = 1$ iff $A(t)$
has full Pauli support on site $k$, $w_k = 0$ iff $A(t)$ is identity on
site $k$, and $\sum_k w_k \in [1, n]$ is the "average Pauli weight" of
$A(t)$. At $t = 0$ with $A = \sigma^x_5$ we have $w_5 = 1$ and $w_k = 0$
for $k \neq 5$.

The Lieb–Robinson commutator bound translates to a site-weight envelope as
follows. Take $B$ = single-site Pauli on site $k$. Then $\|[A(t), B]\|$ is
bounded below by the projection of $A(t)$ onto the Paulis that anticommute
with $B$ — the "weight on site $k$". Squaring (Eq. eq:lr-bhv) and
absorbing constants gives the schematic envelope

$$
w_k(t) \;\lesssim\; \min\Bigl(1,\;\; C\,\exp\bigl[-2\bigl(|k - x_0| - v_\mathrm{LR}^\mathrm{tight}\,|t_\mathrm{phys}|\bigr)\bigr]\Bigr),
$$
<!-- \label{eq:wk-envelope} -->

where $x_0 = 5$ is the initial-support site and the LR decay length $\xi
\approx 1$ lattice spacing (the standard choice for spin-$\tfrac{1}{2}$
NN chains) has been absorbed into the prefactor of the exponent. On the
plot we draw:

- A *lightcone* boundary at $|k - x_0| = v_\mathrm{LR}^\mathrm{tight}\,
  |t_\mathrm{phys}|$, inside which we expect $w_k = O(1)$.
- An *exponential tail* $w_k \lesssim \exp[-2(|k - x_0| -
  v_\mathrm{LR}^\mathrm{tight}\,|t_\mathrm{phys}|)]$ outside the lightcone.

The constant $C$ absorbs $\|A\|$, $\|B\|$, and the bond/site geometry; for
single-site $\sigma^x_5$ with $B = $ single-site Pauli on site $k$, $C =
O(1)$ and is dropped from the plot in favour of a simple unit-anchored
envelope.

## Interpretation (hypothesis check)

The qf-vr4 hypothesis was: *at small system sizes ($n \le 9$), for the
largest relevantly-contributing TimeDomain $t$-label $t_\max$, the
Lieb–Robinson lightcone $v_\mathrm{LR}\,t_\max$ exceeds the chain length,
so $A(t_\max)$ is essentially global on this chain.* On the canonical
$\beta_\mathrm{phys}$ grid the resolution is split:

- **$\beta_\mathrm{phys} = 0.25$ — hypothesis REFUTED.** $v_\mathrm{LR}\,
  t_\max^\mathrm{phys} = 3.81 < 8 = n - 1$, so even at the longest OFT
  time the simulator visits, the lightcone covers fewer than half the
  sites. The site-weight profile is correspondingly localised near
  $k = 5$, with $\sum_k w_k = 3.57$ — the average Pauli weight of $A(t)$
  is roughly $4$, well below the chain length.
- **$\beta_\mathrm{phys} = 0.5$ — hypothesis CONFIRMED.** $v_\mathrm{LR}\,
  t_\max^\mathrm{phys} = 8.17 \gtrsim 8$, the lightcone just reaches the
  chain boundary, and the average Pauli weight is $5.40$.
- **$\beta_\mathrm{phys} = 1.0$ — hypothesis CONFIRMED, decisively.**
  $v_\mathrm{LR}\,t_\max^\mathrm{phys} = 16.13 \approx 2(n - 1)$, the
  lightcone wraps around the chain on either side of $x_0$, and the
  spread saturates at $\sum_k w_k \approx 5.64$ (constant across $k$ on
  the figure, modulo the small variation from the specific seed-46
  disorder draw).

The crossover sits near $\beta_\mathrm{phys} \approx 0.5$ for $n = 9$,
which means the locality intuition for the CKG sampler is *cell-dependent*
on the canonical $\beta_\mathrm{phys}$ grid: $A(t)$ truncates to a small
patch at $\beta_\mathrm{phys} = 0.25$ but is effectively global at
$\beta_\mathrm{phys} = 1$ for the chain sizes the thesis numerics reports.
Any locality-flavoured argument in the chapter (e.g.\ "the OFT integrates
only over a small temporal window, so each jump touches only a few sites")
needs that crossover noted explicitly.

## Why this matters for the numerics chapter

The chapter never claims locality as a quantitative prediction — but
implicit locality intuition does inform sentences like "the dissipator is
quasi-local in $A$". The above shows that this intuition only holds at
high temperature; at $\beta_\mathrm{phys} \ge 1$ on $n \le 9$ the
operator-spread lightcone exceeds the chain length, so the simulator is
*not* effectively local and there is no "small patch" truncation that
would simplify the analysis. The appendix figure provides a clean visual
caveat for this in the form of a single $n = 9$ panel: at the canonical
cell that actually matters for the cold-side numerics, $A(t)$ has spread
across the chain.

A complementary observation is that this is *not a deficiency of the
sampler* — it is a property of the underlying $H$ together with the choice
of $\sigma \propto 1/\beta_\mathrm{alg}$ in the CKG construction.
Tightening the kernel ($\sigma \to 0$) would shrink the OFT window, but at
the cost of resolution in $\gamma(\omega)$ near each Bohr frequency — the
trade-off that motivates the qf-e4z.35 $\sigma$-sweep.
