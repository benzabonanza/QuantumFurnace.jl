# Coherent B quadrature error — TimeDomain vs BohrDomain ideal (qf-b4d.1, qf-dnb)

## Setup

Compare `B_time(jumps, ham, b_-, b_+, t0_b-, t0_b+, β, σ)` vs `B_bohr(ham, jumps, cfg)` in operator norm.
Half-windows: `T_- = 10.0` (outer, b_-), `T_+ = 5.0` (inner, b_+).
Equivalent DFT pairing: `w0_b± = π/T_±`, `t0_b± = 2T_±/2^r`.

## Discovery — slope-(-1) is from the t=0 L'Hôpital sample, scales with t0', not η

**Kinky (s=0) and smooth (s=0.25) Metropolis show identical convergence in time-domain B**:
slope -1 in `2^r_b±` confirmed at every (n, β) in our sweep. The empirical constant
`K = err · 2^(r_b±−1)` at β=10 is mildly n-dependent and *favorable* in n: it shrinks
from `K ≈ 0.036` (n=3) to `0.026` (n=4), `0.020` (n=5), `0.013` (n=6) — about a 3× reduction
across n ∈ {3..6}, so the recipe `r_b± ≥ log_2(2K/ε)` gets cheaper at larger n. The
qf-dnb extension confirms the slope structure is n-stable; only the prefactor drifts.

**Origin of the slope.** Convention used everywhere below: `η < t0' = 2T_+/2^r_b+`. With this
choice the η-cutoff branch of `_compute_b_plus_metro` is **dead code in the discretisation** —
no grid sample falls inside `(-η, η)` except `t = 0`, and at `t = 0` the implementation returns
the L'Hôpital limit `b_+(0) = (2 - σ²β²(1+s))/(2√2π²) ≈ 0.027` regardless of the η-branch.

The slope-(-1) error vs `B_bohr` is then
$$
\|B_\text{bohr} - B_\text{time}\|_\text{op} \;\sim\; t_0' \cdot |b_+(0)| \cdot \|\textstyle\sum_a A^{a\dagger}A^a\| \cdot \|b_-\|_1 \;+\; \mathcal{O}(t_0'^2),
$$
i.e. **the trap-rule includes a contribution `t0' · b_+(0) · K^a(0)` from the single t=0 sample
that the Cauchy P.V. integral defining `B^(s)` explicitly excludes**. This is the dominant
discretisation error. The η-jump rate `T_+²/(N_+·η²)` from thesis eq:r_+_metro is for the
opposite regime `t0' < η` which we never use; under our convention indicator and any smooth-bump
replacement evaluate to identical kernel values at every grid sample.

**Implication for r_b± scaling.** Setting `K · t0'/T_+ = K · 2/2^r_b± ≤ ε`:
$$
r_b\pm \;\geq\; \log_2(2K/\varepsilon) \;\approx\; \log_2(0.07/\varepsilon)
$$
— **single log in 1/ε, η-independent**. Concretely r_b± = 6 reaches ε=1e-3, r_b± ≈ 15 reaches
the η-bias floor (~1.5e-6 at η = 2.22e-4 in our fixture, much smaller than the loose
Background-1 bound `O(η·β·‖H‖)`).

For the Gaussian filter no L'Hôpital sample is needed (no 1/t pole in `b_+^(G)`), so the
trap-rule rate is the smooth Gevrey-class super-algebraic — saturates at machine precision
for r_b± ≥ 6.
