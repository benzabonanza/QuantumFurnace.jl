## A note on multi-rank DLL

The standard DLL of [Ding et al. 2024 §3.1][CITE: ding2025efficient] uses one filter $q^a$ per coupling, giving a rank-$1$ Kossakowski $\alpha^{\text{DLL}}_{\nu_1, \nu_2} = e^{-\beta(\nu_1 + \nu_2)/4}\,q^a(\nu_1)\,q^a(\nu_2)^\ast$. A natural question is whether allowing $k > 1$ symmetrised filter channels per coupling — for example via the translates
$$
q_\ell(\nu) \;=\; \sqrt{w/2}\,\bigl[q_{\text{base}}(\nu - \nu_\ell) + q_{\text{base}}(\nu + \nu_\ell)\bigr], \qquad \ell = 1,\dots,k,
$$
parametrised by centers $\nu_\ell$ uniformly spaced on $[0, c_{\max}]$ — can recover any of the parallelism that gives CKG its many-channel mixing.

We tried this on the rescaled $n=3$ fixture at $\beta\in\{5,10,20\}$, $k\in\{1,2,4,8\}$, and four choices of $c_{\max}$, measuring (i) $\tau_{\text{mix}}$, (ii) the LW23 block-encoding aggregator $\|\mathcal{L}\|_{\text{be}} = \alpha_G + \tfrac12 \alpha_L^2 \,|\mathcal{A}|\,k$, and (iii) the scale-invariant intrinsic mixing ratio $\rho_{\text{intrinsic}} = \lambda(\mathcal{L}) / \Lambda_{\max}(\mathcal{L})$ (Sec. [intrinsic-ratio]). The results give no clear evidence of a *structural* mixing-time gain over rank-$1$ DLL. At $\beta = 20$, $\rho_{\text{intrinsic}}$ is approximately preserved at small $c_{\max}$ (DLL k=1: $0.157$ → DLL k=8, $c_{\max}=0.1$: $0.147$, both slightly below CKG's $0.163$) and *worsens* at larger $c_{\max}$ (down to $0.036$ at $c_{\max} = \nu_{\max}(H)$). What does grow with $k$ is the Lindbladian itself: $\|\mathcal{L}\|_{\text{op}}$ grows roughly as $k\cdot\|\mathcal{L}^{\text{CKG}}\|_{\text{op}}$ (factor $14.5$ at $k = 8$, $c_{\max} = 0.1$, $\beta = 20$) — so the $\tau_{\text{mix}}$ improvement we observe is consistent with uniform rescale of the generator, not with a structural advantage. The same effect is visible in the block-encoding aggregator: $\|\mathcal{L}\|_{\text{be}}\cdot\tau_{\text{mix}}$ never falls below the rank-$1$ baseline in any cell of the sweep (closest case: $1.25\times$ worse at $\beta = 20$, $c_{\max} = 0.1$, $k = 8$), and exceeds it by orders of magnitude once $c_{\max}$ passes the Hamiltonian's Bohr support, where each shifted channel pays a $\cosh^2(\beta\,c_{\max}/4)$ penalty inherited from pulling the KMS factor through the $\nu$-shift.

A *parametrisation-independent* lower bound clarifies what every additional channel pays. Any DLL channel of the form (Eq. 3.4 of [Ding et al.][CITE: ding2025efficient]) with weighting function $q$ satisfying KMS Hermiticity $q(-\nu) = \overline{q(\nu)}$ has time-domain weight $f(t)$ obeying
$$
\|f\|_{L^1} \;\ge\; \|\hat f\|_{L^\infty} \;\ge\; |q(\nu_0)|\,e^{\beta\nu_0/4} \quad \text{for every } \nu_0 > 0 \text{ in supp}(q),
$$ <!-- \label{eq:dll-channel-l1-lower} -->
since $|\hat f(\omega)| \le \|f\|_{L^1}$ at $\omega = -\nu_0$ together with $|q(-\nu_0)| = |q(\nu_0)|$. The Kossakowski's *useful* antidiagonal mass at $(\nu_0, -\nu_0)$ is $\alpha(\nu_0, -\nu_0) = |q(\nu_0)|^2$, so any channel that drives population transfer at Bohr-gap $\nu_0$ must pay at least $|q(\nu_0)|\,e^{\beta\nu_0/4}$ in the LCU 1-norm of its time-domain weight. Eq. dll-channel-l1-lower depends only on the KMS-Hermiticity constraint, not on how $q$ is parametrised; the $\cosh(\beta\nu_\ell/4)$ in our shifted-translate construction saturates it up to a factor of $2$. This is the structural fact behind the qualitative behaviour we observe: redistributing a fixed total Kossakowski budget across more channels (e.g., the eigendecomposition route of [Ding et al. 2024 §4][CITE: ding2025efficient], $\widetilde C = QDQ^T$, which embeds CKG as a special case via $r \approx |B_H|$) can dilute the per-channel exponential by spreading $q$ across many $\nu$-entries, but it cannot remove it; adding channels at *full* per-channel strength (our shifted translates, or any additive construction) makes both $\|\mathcal{L}\|_{\text{op}}$ and the block-encoding aggregator grow with $r$.

What we can say, then, is twofold. First, for the parametric family we tried, the algorithmic figure of merit does not improve and structural $\rho_{\text{intrinsic}}$ stays at or below rank-$1$. Second, any *other* multi-rank construction faces the same Eq. dll-channel-l1-lower bound: a structurally faster multi-rank DLL would have to deliver its mixing-time gain *in spite of* growing block-encoding norms — which translate directly into more circuit complexity per unit of Lindbladian evolution under the LW23 simulation cost — and we have no evidence that any such construction exists at scales beyond $\sim 1.4\times$ in $\rho_{\text{intrinsic}}$ (the empirical CKG-vs-rank-$1$-DLL gap at $n \le 5$, $\beta \le 20$). We therefore restrict the rest of this comparison to rank-$1$ DLL Metropolis vs CKG smooth-Metropolis, where the trade-off is cleanest: comparable $\tau_{\text{mix}}$ (within $\sim 25\%$, see [previous subsection]), comparable $\|\mathcal{L}\|_{\text{op}}$ (ratio $0.99$–$1.00$ across the grid), but markedly different block-encoding cost — DLL's coherent term $G$ scales linearly in $\beta$ ([Ding et al. Remark 23][CITE: ding2025efficient]) while CKG's stays logarithmic in $\beta$ ([Chen et al. 2025, Cor. III.2][CITE: chen2023efficient]).
